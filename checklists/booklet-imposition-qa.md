# Booklet imposition QA checklist

Use this checklist before exporting or printing an imposed booklet. It is written for operators who already have a PDF and need a fast way to catch the common booklet mistakes: page count, reading order, duplex orientation, creep, marks, bleed, and trim. To build the booklet in the browser, use [https://www.pdfpress.app/booklet-maker](https://www.pdfpress.app/booklet-maker).

## 1. Confirm the binding method

Decide whether the job is saddle-stitch, perfect-bound, or another signature workflow before arranging pages. Saddle-stitch booklets are nested and usually need a page count divisible by four. Perfect-bound work often uses signatures that are folded separately and then gathered. The binding method determines page order, creep behavior, spine treatment, and whether blank pages are acceptable.

## 2. Check page count and blank pages

Count the source pages before imposition. If the source page count is not compatible with the binding method, decide where blanks should appear. Do not let software insert unknown blanks without reviewing them. A blank inside front cover, inside back cover, notes page, or intentional ad space may be acceptable; a random blank in the middle of a program is usually not.

## 3. Preview imposed order

Scroll through the imposed sheets and mentally fold the first few sheets. Check front and back sides. For saddle-stitch work, page 1 should pair with the last page on the first outside spread. For longer books, sample the first sheet, middle sheet, and final sheet. In PDF Press, start from the [booklet maker](https://www.pdfpress.app/booklet-maker) and use the preview before downloading.

## 4. Validate duplex orientation

Many booklet failures are duplex failures. Confirm whether the printer flips on the long edge or short edge. If backs are upside down, fix the duplex setting or the imposition rotation before printing the full run. Print one cheap proof, fold it, and turn every page.

## 5. Review creep and trim risk

Creep pushes inner pages outward in saddle-stitch booklets. If the booklet has enough pages for visible creep, enable compensation and check that page numbers, footers, and narrow margins do not drift into the trim. For a deeper explanation, read [https://www.pdfpress.app/blog/booklet-creep-compensation-guide](https://www.pdfpress.app/blog/booklet-creep-compensation-guide).

## 6. Check bleed, marks, and safe area

Confirm that artwork extends beyond trim where needed. Check crop marks, registration marks, and any color bars or sluglines. Keep marks outside the finished trim and make sure there is enough sheet margin for the printer and cutter. If the source PDF has no bleed, review whether mirrored bleed or a redesign is safer before printing.

## 7. Export and archive the final PDF

After proofing, download the imposed PDF, name it with job, size, date, and version, then keep the source PDF separately. This makes it possible to re-run the job or prove what was sent to print.
